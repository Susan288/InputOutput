PhD Research: Effectiveness of Proactive Healthcare

About the Research

The research explores the impact and implementation of proactive healthcare systems, focusing on prevention rather than cure. It investigates the effectiveness of early interventions, patient education, digital health monitoring, and predictive analytics in improving overall health outcomes and reducing long-term healthcare costs.

Objectives

  • To evaluate the effectiveness of preventive healthcare strategies.
  • To study the role of digital tools in early diagnosis and monitoring.
  • To assess patient response to proactive health interventions.
  • To propose a framework for integrating proactive methods into traditional healthcare systems.

Key Findings

Preliminary findings suggest that proactive healthcare approaches significantly reduce hospital admissions, improve quality of life, and promote long-term cost savings for both patients and providers.
